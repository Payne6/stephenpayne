STEVE PAYNE
WORK ABOUT CONTACT CV

Steve Payne works from a studio based in central Birmingham.

His work has engaged with most of the traditional subjects of Western European Art, using these as a springboard into bodies of work which meld tradition with a highly personal, sometimes idiosyncratic approach to both image and method.

An early body of work dealt with the interplay of vehicle and human traffic in the urban landscape, a long series of drawing and paintings on paper heavily influenced by living for three years in Rochdale and a deliberate break from previous work which was more illustrative in character.

A move to Birmingham and a larger studio led to more ambitious work with greater experimentation in media, methods and materials with the focus on still-life and interiors deploying the domestic environment as a microcosm of a larger consumer-driven society, an ironic survey of the underclass of disposable, throwaway objects and materials.

Paintings of small, single objects set against a dark ground extended some of these ideas into more ambiguous forms; brooding expressive paintings, subtly ironic and more abstract.

A return to the studio after a long absence ushered in a series of small narrative paintings, evoking rather than describing events and situations, and deliberately ambiguous as to any fixed interpretation.

Recent work has focussed on landscape but now more rural, relatively neglected areas of hinterland, featureless, not obviously inhabited, though bearing the traces of human or animal activity; smaller in scale and mostly explored through drawing.

The website acts as a survey of some of the most significant work outlined above.
