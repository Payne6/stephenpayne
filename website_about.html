STEVE PAYNE
WORK ABOUT CONTACT CV

Steve Payne is an artist based in Kenilworth with a studio in Central Birmingham.

His work has engaged with most of the traditional subjects of Western European Art, using these as a springboard into bodies of work which meld tradition with a highly personal, sometimes idiosyncratic approach to both image and method.

Within the apparent diversity of themes much of the imagery deals with how we engage with our various environments whether on the land, in the city or at home.

Recurring preoccupations have included:

an urban landscape designed to control traffic flow, where car users become a different species from pedestrians in an almost arbitrary distinction of one mode of being human from another

the domestic environment as a microcosm of a larger consumer-driven society inundated with clutter; a world where the human presence is displaced to the margins by the predatory encroachment of everyday objects; the living room as landfill

the landscape of farmland, hinterland, tracks and trails; featureless, but bearing traces of human or animal activity

the story-telling of early Renaissance painting and the social and political commentaries of some 20th century art

a fusion of image and medium

drawing as the bedrock: where ideas are generated, tested, defined, refined and reviewed. Often an autonomous practice, creating material with no further purpose, fragmentary, provisional, spontaneous, and suggestive; visual thinking at its most basic and productive.

Recent exhibitions have included the John Moores Painting Prize 2018, and The Trinity Buoy Wharf Drawing Prize 2018, 2019 and 2021.

His work is held in public collections including Birmingham City Museum and Art Gallery, Touchstones (Rochdale), and numerous private collections in the UK, Europe, Canada and Australia.
